About

I am a PhD candidate at the Centre de Recherche en Automatique de Nancy (CRAN), Université de Lorraine, where my research sits at the intersection of control systems engineering and reinforcement learning.

My work focuses on developing safe, optimal control algorithms with Lyapunov-based guarantees for nonlinear autonomous systems — with applications in robotics and aerospace. I contribute to the ANR-funded SOS project, targeting intelligent drone fleets for forest fire-fighting missions.

Prior to my PhD, I studied at ISAE-SUPAERO in Toulouse and completed my undergraduate degree in Aerospace Engineering in India. I enjoy bridging rigorous mathematical control theory with practical learning-based methods.
